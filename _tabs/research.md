# Analysis of Data Bias and Generalization Limitations in NER Systems Based on Psychological Counseling Datasets

🏆 Excellent Paper Award  
KICSP 2026 Spring Conference

**Authors**
- Eunbin Ha
- Jeong-hoon Shin

**Affiliation**
- School of Computer Software, Daegu Catholic University

---

## Abstract

This study investigates data bias and generalization limitations in Named Entity Recognition (NER) systems trained on psychological counseling datasets.

Three major sources of bias were identified:

- Label imbalance
- Topic bias
- Domain-specific generalization limitations

---

## Research Areas

- Natural Language Processing (NLP)
- Named Entity Recognition (NER)
- Counseling NLP
- Data Bias Analysis
- Domain Generalization

---

## Method

- Label imbalance analysis
- Topic bias analysis
- Domain generalization analysis
- Synthetic data augmentation

The proposed approach analyzes bias in psychological counseling datasets and evaluates methods to improve model robustness.

---

## Results

- Micro F1 improved from **59.07%** to **84.30%**.
- Significant improvements were observed in minority labels such as **CAUSE** and **PER2**.
- Domain generalization remains an important challenge for real-world applications.
